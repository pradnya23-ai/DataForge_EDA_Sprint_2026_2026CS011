
## 1.What does one row represent?
Ans: One row represents a single record/observation (e.g., one customer, transaction, or event depending on the dataset).


## 2.Which columns are numerical, categorical, and date/time?
Ans: Numerical: columns with numbers used for calculations (e.g., price, age, quantity)
Categorical: columns with labels or groups (e.g., city, gender, status)
Date/Time: columns showing dates or timestamps (e.g., order date, login time)


## 3. What can go wrong without a data dictionary?
Ans: The team may misunderstand column meanings, use wrong data types, make inconsistent assumptions, and produce incorrect or unreliable analysis results.
